COUNTING THE STARS AT NIGHT

By Yun Dong Ju
(1941)

And ready I wait without worry
to count all the stars she bears

Now the reason I cannot tally
all the stars impressed on my heart,
is ‘cause the morning soon comes,
my youth’s not quite done,
and another night still lays in store.

One star for memories

One star for loving

One star for melancholy

Another for longing

One star for poetry

Another for ma, mother

육사, 소월, 몽규
The names of school friends I sat with,
패, 경, 옥
Pae, Kyeong and Oak;
these foreign girls' names
순자, 영자, 정희
The names of the girls
who are already mothers of babies,

Mother

I will try to name all the stars
after beautiful words:

영순, 정훈, 춘자
other poor neighboring folk; the pigeons, the puppies,
비둘기, 강아지,
토끼, 노새, 노루
pigeons, puppies, rabbits, mules, roe deer,
프랑시스잠, 라이너마리아릴케
the names of such poets as
"Francis Jam", "Liner Maria Rilke"

And mother,

you are far away, in North Gando.

Pining for something
I scribble my name into a star spattered hill.
Then bury it (again.)

As for the bird who wails through the night
on account of the pain of its name full of shame

But winter will pass bringing spring to my star.
As the tuft grows round gravestones the grass will abound
where my name has been buried in that star spattered mound.
